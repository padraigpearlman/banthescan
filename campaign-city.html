New York City Campaign

Three bills. One city ban.

Three bills at the New York City Council would ban biometric surveillance in public accommodations, residential buildings, and by city government itself. Here's what each bill does, and how to tell your Council Member to pass them.

The Legislation

What these three bills actually do.

Two of the three bills have already been introduced at the City Council. The third, a ban on city government's own use of biometric surveillance, is still being drafted, which is exactly why it needs public pressure now.

Int 0213-2026 Introduced, In Committee

Ban on Biometric Surveillance in Public Accommodations

Sponsored by Council Member Shahana Hanif

Prohibits stores, arenas, and other places of public accommodation in New York City from using facial recognition or biometric surveillance to identify, track, or exclude customers, directly responding to Madison Square Garden barring attorneys at the door and retailers scanning shoppers without consent.

Read the Bill Text → View the Biometric Map → Relevant Media → Memo of Support (PDF) →
Int 0428-2026 Introduced

Ban on Biometric Surveillance by Residential Landlords

Sponsored by Council Member Pierina Sanchez

Prohibits NYC landlords from requiring tenants to use facial recognition or other biometric systems to enter their own buildings, and bars collection of biometric data as a condition of tenancy.

Read the Bill Text → Relevant Media → Memo of Support (PDF) →
Not Yet Numbered Not Yet Introduced

Ban on Government Use of Biometric Surveillance

Sponsor: TBD

Would prohibit New York City agencies, including the NYPD, from using facial recognition and biometric surveillance, closing the accountability gap exposed by the NYPD's 22,000+ facial recognition searches between 2016 and 2019. This bill needs a Council sponsor and public pressure to move forward.
